How to use the calculator?

Enter the course name, grade, credit, and course type of of each of your courses. To add more courses, click the Add Course button. To remove a course, click on the ‚ìç beside the course. If you do not know the credit of a course, set it to 1.

Note that the course name does not affect the GPA.

You can also change the Grade's Key by just tapping on the Edit Grade's Key button under the Grade's Key. If there are no + and - in letter grades, uncheck the + and - letters. Here are the rules of the Garde's Key:

  • A GPA of a higher grade letter cannot be greater than that of a lower grade letter.
  • The percentage grade is divided so that it is minimum to maximum grade where minimum grade is inclusive and maximum grade is exclusive, except the first maximum grade.
  • The maximum and minimum percentage of the same letter cannot equal to each other
  • The maximum percentage of a letter must be greater than the minimum percentage of that same letter.
  • The maximum percentage of a lower grade letter must equal to minimum percentage of the letter above it.

What is GPA?

GPA stands for Grade Average Point. It is a form of way to evaluate grades. It is usually used on a 4.0 scaling point, meaning that the GPA is calculated out of 4.

What is CGPA?

CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Average Point. It is the average of all GPAs achieved. For example, CGPA can be calculated by getting the average between the first school term and the second school term GPAs. Another example is getting the average GPA from grade 9 through 12.

What is the difference between weighted and unweighted GPA?

Weighted GPA gives more grades to AP, honors, IB, and college courses. Unweighted GPA does not give any more grades to them. Weighted GPA gives 0.5 GPA more to honors courses and 1 GPA more to AP, IB, and college courses. Make sure with your school if they give out weighted, unweighted, or both GPA.

How do you calculate unweighted GPA?

The calculation is actually simple.

  1. Get the grade percentage or letter of each course and use the Grade's Key to get each course's GPA.
  2. Multiply each GPA by the amount of credit of the course. Each course is usually 1 credit hour.
  3. Add all the GPAs of all the courses together.
  4. Divide the sum by the total of credit hours taken.
  5. Voila! You have your GPA!
Note that the Grade's Key can vary from school to school.

How do you calculate weighted GPA?

Do the same calculation for as the unweighted GPA, but make sure that after step 1 you add 0.5 GPA to honors courses and 1 GPA to AP, IB, or college courses.

What is AP?

AP stands for Advanced Placement. It is an series of exams made by the College Board that a student can choose from. They give a change for the student to take college-level work while they are still in school. They can sometimes be exchanged fro college credits. For more information, visit the AP website.
